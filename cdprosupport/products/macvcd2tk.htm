Video CD 2.0 Toolkit for Macintosh



pmpro sells a Video CD development package (also makes CD-i presentations) for Macintosh and Macintosh Power PC computers. The Video CD development package has everything you need to make Video CD/CD-i training, educational, presentation, and testing applications. The Toolkit supports ALL the White Book 2.0 features, including still pictures, PCM and MPEG audio, MPEG moving video, menus, closed captioning, random menu selections and timeout menus.

Authoring Features:
  • Drag and drop for easy creation of events, sequences and menus
  • Mini-windows for easy access and organization of menus, events, assets, tracks and navigation trees
  • Can import as templates, other projects made with this tool
  • Automatically converts menu and slide pictures into required MPEG stills
  • Recognizes many Mac picture formats, including PICT, TGA, TIFF
  • Automatically assigns Video CD player track and item selections as you create menu items
  • Automatically converts PCM, AIFF and SDII audio files for use as CD-DA tracks
  • Automatically creates all necessary files and components required by the White Book 2.0 CD specification
  • Automatically creates the disc image file necessary for recording to a CD-R
  • Analyzes your menus, sequences and assets for compliance to the VCD spec

Note: Moving video must be converted to Video CD MPEG-1, BEFORE bringing it to Toolkit. Audio files must be converted to MPEG-audio BEFORE bringing them to Toolkit. VCD 2.0 does not offer a variety of video effects--all transitions are "cuts".

Bundle Includes:
  • Full-featured Video CD Toolkit software
  • Unlimited distribution license for the CD-i Application for up to 500 copies per title
  • CD recording software
  • CD recorder (external SCSI)
  • Ten blank CD-R's
  • CD-i Player with Digital Video Cartridge
  • One year of phone/fax/email development support


Minimum Development Requirements:
  • System 7.5.x
  • x040 or Power PC
  • 15 inch monitor
  • 2 GB of FREE hard drive space
  • 16 MB RAM
  • SCSI CD-R recorder
  • CD-i or VCD player to test the Video CD's with
  • VCD 2.0 compliant MPEG-1 encoder or MPEG-1 service facility to process video tape content
